The flowers of jasmine also commonly called poet's jasmine are known for their mild fragrance and white blooms, while the flowers of Arabian Jasmine are pearly white, waxy in texture, and have a strong fragrance.Arabian Jasmine is a highly fragrant and exotic flower botanically known as Jasminum Sambac which literally means a gift from God. From age-old times, it has been widely used by Greeks and Egyptians to derive myriad wellness benefits it contains that are not limited to health but also to skin, hair, and sexual health. The beautiful attractive and sweetly fragrant flowers of this plant are used for perfumes, oils, and herbal teas besides curing ailments. It is also a potential aphrodisiac as well as a natural stimulant. The paste of Arabian Jasmine extract is used to treat healing sores and scars. A small shrub growing up to 3 meters in height, apparently, Arabian Jasmine is the national flower of the Philippines. Also known by many vernacular names such as Maid of Orleans, Belle of India, Grand Duke of Tuscany, Mogra in Hindi, Gundu malligai poo in Tamil and Mallige in Kannada, adding this aromatic flower into your diet or using topically can benefit your health in many ways.This flower has a special mention in ayurvedic texts for the fact that Arabian Jasmine possesses antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties and has been valued as an effective remedy for various health ailments such as headaches, nausea, impotence, heart ailments, epilepsy, ulcers, and eye disorders. Full of flavonoid antioxidants that are known to fight oxidative stress, it carries mild sedative property that calms the mind. The flower is also very popular in Chinese medicine and in fact, they adore jasmine in their teas.
